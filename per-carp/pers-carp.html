General informations

Persian carpet (in persian: qali irani) is Persia's uniqe carpet, which has been produced in hundreds of years for a variety of different utilitarian and symbolic purposes in Iran, Afghanistan,Tajikistan and the other territories which has once been a part of Persia. Persian carpet and rugs have deep cultural influence in persian countries and can be found in almost any Persian home.

History

The origins of persian carpets is unknown since, carpets are subjects to use, destruction and insects, but we know that they were first mentioned by the greek author Xenophon in 500BC, which clearly shows the ancienety of Persian carpets.

Classification

Persian carpets are best classified by the social context of their weavers. Carpets were produced simultaneously by nomadic tribes, in villages, town and court manufactures, for home use, local sale, or export. They are classified into 6 main classes which are:

  • Kurds
  • Bakhtyari and Lori
  • Chahar Mahal
  • Qashqaee and Khamesh
  • Afshari
  • Baluch
